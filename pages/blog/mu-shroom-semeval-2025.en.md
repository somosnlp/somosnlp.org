## Full Invitation

We are pleased to announce Mu-SHROOM, a shared task to improve hallucination detection methods ([visit our page](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://helsinki-nlp.github.io/shroom/&sa=D&source=editors&ust=1729608218612132&usg=AOvVaw09zWPQku6EBau7DyWuJch1)). We invite participants to detect text fragments that are hallucinations in texts generated by state-of-the-art multilingual LLMs (Large Language Models).


### Introduction

We designed Mu-SHROOM based on lessons learned from the first iteration of our hallucination shared task: [SHROOM](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://helsinki-nlp.github.io/shroom/2024&sa=D&source=editors&ust=1729608218612605&usg=AOvVaw1plQWBkDuiGhcyDhtf_NhR). Mu-SHROOM incorporates three fundamental improvements: 1) a focus on large language models (LLMs), 2) multilingual annotations in 10 languages, and 3) the task now consists of predicting the text fragments that are hallucinations.


LLMs frequently produce "hallucinations," i.e., they generate plausible but incorrect text. Furthermore, current evaluation metrics prioritize text fluency over correctness. This gives rise to an increasingly concerning problem as these models are more widely adopted by the public.


With Mu-SHROOM, we want to promote the development of methods for detecting hallucinated content. This new iteration of the shared task takes place in a multilingual and multi-model context. In other words, we provide data produced by a variety of open LLMs in 10 different languages (Arabic (Modern Standard), Chinese (Mandarin), English, Finnish, French, German, Hindi, Italian, Spanish, and Swedish).


We invite research groups to participate in any of the available languages. To participate, they must develop systems capable of accurately identifying and mitigating hallucinations in LLM-generated content.

As is customary in SemEval, participants will be invited to submit research papers describing their systems, with the option to present them at the upcoming SemEval 2025 workshop (which will coincide with one of the upcoming *ACL conferences). Participants who choose to write a system description paper must also participate in paper review processes (maximum 2 papers per author).

### Evaluation Metrics:

We will use two metrics:

1. IoU (Intersection-over-Union): the intersection of characters marked as hallucinations in the annotated reference over those characters predicted as hallucinations
2. The correlation between the assigned probability that a character is part of a hallucination and the empirical probabilities observed in our annotations.

Rankings will be made independently per language: you can participate in whichever languages interest you!
